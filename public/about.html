About Us

Udeaku CareSpring Support Foundation

We are a non-profit organization committed to promoting compassion, health education, and community support especially among young people and vulnerable communities.


Udeaku CareSpring Support Foundation focuses on empowering individuals with essential life-saving skills, health awareness, and humanitarian values. Our journey began with a passion to extend care beyond words through practical action and meaningful impact.

Since inception, we have carried out impactful initiatives such as the Pad a Girl campaign, providing menstrual hygiene support for girls, and the payment of hospital bills for patients in need.

These programs reflect our belief in standing in the gap for others and giving hope where it‚Äôs most needed. We aim to equip communities, schools, and individuals with knowledge that saves lives and fosters a culture of care.

Aims & Objectives

Why Udeaku CareSpring Exists

Udeaku CareSpring Support Foundation responds to a real and urgent need in Nigeria: life-saving knowledge, compassionate care, and community support. Our aims and objectives guide every training, outreach, and act of kindness.

01

Promote first aid education & awareness

To promote first aid education and awareness among schools, youth, and communities across Nigeria, addressing the country‚Äôs critical need for life-saving knowledge as highlighted by the World Health Organization (WHO), which reports Nigeria among the lowest in life expectancy globally due to preventable causes and lack of first aid awareness.

We bring first aid training closer to people. We are turning fear into confidence and panic into preparedness through structured Workshops and outreach activities.

02

Enhance community health & safety

To enhance community health and safety through public enlightenment programs focused on hygiene, preventive care, and emergency response readiness.

03

Build meaningful partnerships

To establish partnerships with local and international organizations that share our vision of improving health, humanitarian service, and access to first aid education.

04

Inspire compassionate action

To create opportunities for volunteers and caregivers to demonstrate compassion and social responsibility through active participation in community care initiatives.

05

Promote a culture of care

To promote a culture of care, compassion, and service to humanity, inspiring young individuals to contribute toward building a safer and healthier society.

From awareness to action

Udeaku CareSpring exists as a bridge between compassion and practical support. Each Workshop, school visit, or outreach program is crafted to turn knowledge into life-saving ability.

By teaching first aid and nurturing a culture of care, we help communities respond better to emergencies, prevent avoidable deaths, and uphold dignity in times of crisis.

The Heart Behind Udeaku CareSpring Foundation
Our Founder
Tourism & Hospitality Management | Practicing Nurse | Humanitarian service
Precious-Winifred Udeaku Onyenaobiya
Founder, Udeaku CareSpring Support Foundation


Precious-Winifred is a compassionate and purpose-driven woman whose life experiences shaped her vision for the Udeaku CareSpring Support Foundation.

With a background in Tourism & Hospitality Management and professional experience in nursing and caregiving, she brings both empathy and organization to her humanitarian work. Her journey toward founding Udeaku CareSpring was inspired by personal loss. An experience that deepened her desire to help others, provide comfort, and share hope with those in need.

Through Udeaku CareSpring, she has led initiatives like the Pad a Girl Program and medical bill support projects, reaching individuals and families who would have struggled in silence. She founded Udeaku CareSpring Support Foundation to serve as a bridge between compassion and structured support.

She continues to lead with grace, faith, and a firm belief that one person's compassion can spark change in an entire community.



Udeaku CareSpring Support Foundation is more than an organization; it is an answer to a deep longing for hope, care, and practical support in communities where these are most needed.

Under Precious-Winifred's leadership, Udeaku CareSpring stands as a bridge between empathy and action transforming compassion into structured programs that educate, support, and uplift communities.

Caring is not just an act, it's a calling. Every person we teach, every life we touch, brings us closer to a safer, more compassionate world.
